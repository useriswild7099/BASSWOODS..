Sustainability

Leave No Trace

Basswoods operates in some of India's last pristine biological hotspots. Our presence must be invisible. We follow the 7 international principles of Leave No Trace:

01

Plan & Prepare

Know the regulations. Avoid high-use areas during sensitive times. Repackage food to minimize waste.

02

Travel & Camp on Durable Surfaces

Concentrate use on existing trails and campsites. Keep campsites small.

03

Dispose of Waste Properly

Pack it in, pack it out. Deposit human waste in catholes at least 200ft from water.

Camping Logistics FAQ

What is the check-in time for festival camps?

Standard check-in is 2:00 PM. Early check-in can be requested but is subject to site availability.

Are pets allowed in the campsites?

Usually yes, but with strict controls. Pets must remain leashed at all times to protect local wildlife and ensure other guest comfort. A nominal pet cleaning fee may apply.

Guest Protection

Expedition Security Protocol

1. Perimeter Control

Every BASSWOODS camp features a multi-tiered security layer. We collaborate with village 'Gao-Burras' (Heads) to ensure that the surrounding communities are stakeholders in your safety. 24-hour internal patrols are standard across all major festival campsites.

2. Wildlife Mitigation

In remote Arunachal and Assam hubs, we use non-invasive acoustic sensors and fire-barriers to prevent human-wildlife conflict. Our waste-management protocol ensures zero food-scent drift, which is the primary cause of animal attraction.

3. Medical Readiness

Our campsites are equipped with Level-1 Trauma kits and oxygen cylinders for high-altitude zones. Every site lead is certified in Wilderness First Aid (WFA) and has a direct radio link to the nearest regional medical evacuation center.
