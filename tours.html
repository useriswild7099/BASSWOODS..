Learning Center

Frontier Handbook

Northeast India is a collection of complex cultures, unique biomes, and logistical nuances. Our handbook ensures you arrive as an informed, respectful explorer.

1. RAP / PAP / ILP

Navigating the administrative boundaries of the Northeast requires careful planning. Indian citizens require Inner Line Permits (ILP) for states like Arunachal, Nagaland, and Mizoram. International travelers require Restricted Area Permits (RAP) or Protected Area Permits (PAP). BASSWOODS provides free permit coordination for all our booking squads.

Pro Tip: Apply at least 15 days in advance for RAP/PAP as they involve central authority verification.

2. Culinary Heritage

The cuisine of the Northeast is built on fermentation, smoking, and locally-foraged botanicals. From the smoked pork of Nagaland to the bamboo shoot stews of Arunachal, the flavor profile is distinct. We recommend being open to ethnic palate explorations. Our camps feature 'Ethnic Buffet' nights where local home-chefs showcase the homestead flavors.

Must Try: Dohneiiong (Khasi pork with black sesame) and Galho (Naga wholesome rice porridge).

How early should I book for a festival tour?
For major festivals like Ziro or Hornbill, we recommend booking at least 3-4 months in advance. These events attract global crowds and our premium camping slots fill up very quickly.
Is it safe to travel in the Northeast?
Absolutely. The Northeast is incredibly safe and welcoming. BASSWOODS takes safety seriously, providing professional local guides and 24/7 logistical support for all our groups.
What is included in the "Standard" tour rate?
Most packages include high-quality camping gear (tents, mattresses, blankets), breakfast, village tours, guided treks, and logistical transport within the tour area.
