Medical Integrity

High Altitude Protocol

1. Acclimatization Rule

For expeditions exceeding 9,000 ft, we mandate a 24-hour acclimatization period at the basecamp. Our guides monitor SpO2 levels and hydration status. Drink at least 4-5 liters of water daily to combat atmospheric dry air.

2. AMS Recognition

Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS) is a reality in Arunachal. Symptoms include severe headache, nausea, and disorientation. Our guides are trained to recognize these early. If AMS is suspected, immediate descent of at least 2,000 ft is our primary survival protocol.
