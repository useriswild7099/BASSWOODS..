SYSTEM CLASSIFICATION

The Grading Matrix

LEVEL 01

Basecamp

Low incline, defined trails. Suitable for first-time trekkers and families. Max elevation < 2,500m.

LEVEL 02

Cloud-Walker

Moderate daily distance. Significant ridgeline walking. Requires basic aerobic fitness. Max elevation < 3,500m.

LEVEL 03

Ridge-Runner

Steep ascents and unpaved terrain. Multi-day endurance required. Level-2 medical gear standard.

LEVEL 04

Frontier-Elite

Technical scrambling and glacial crossings. Expedition format only. Advanced equipment mandatory.

MEDICAL INTEGRITY

High Altitude Protocol

01. ACCLIMATIZATION

For expeditions exceeding 9,000 ft, we mandate a 24-hour acclimatization period at the basecamp. Our guides monitor SpO2 levels and hydration status using surgical precision.

02. AMS RECOGNITION

Acute Mountain Sickness is a reality in Arunachal. Symptoms include severe headache and disorientation. If AMS is suspected, immediate descent is the primary survival protocol.

LOGISTICS FAQ
What is the guide-to-trekker ratio?

We maintain a 1:5 ratio for moderate treks and 1:3 for high-altitude/winter treks to ensure total safety.

Do you provide personal porters?

Yes, personal porters can be arranged. They carry a maximum of 12kg. We recommend booking 15 days in advance.
