Medical Integrity

High Altitude Protocol

1. Acclimatization Rule

For expeditions exceeding 9,000 ft, we mandate a 24-hour acclimatization period at the basecamp. Our guides monitor SpO2 levels and hydration status. Drink at least 4-5 liters of water daily to combat atmospheric dry air.

2. AMS Recognition

Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS) is a reality in Arunachal. Symptoms include severe headache, nausea, and disorientation. Our guides are trained to recognize these early. If AMS is suspected, immediate descent of at least 2,000 ft is our primary survival protocol.

Bio-Metrics

Alpine Physiology

Trekking in the Eastern Himalayas (Arunachal/Sikkim) demands technical biological awareness. Our physiology protocols are designed for the high-pressure shifts of the alpine frontier.

AMS / HAPE / HAPE PROTOCOLS

SpO2.

Technical Acclimatization

At altitudes above 10,000ft, barometric pressure drops, leading to hypoxic stress. BASSWOODS lead guides monitor SpO2 levels and pulse rates every 6 hours using medical-grade pulse oximeters. Our 'Climb High, Sleep Low' methodology ensures gradual atmospheric adaptation, significantly reducing the risk of Pulmonary or Cerebral Oedema.

SYMPTOM MATRIX

Persistent cough (HAPE), loss of coordination (HACE), severe headache (AMS).

RESPONSE

Immediate descent (1000m minimum), portable hyperbaric bag (Gamow bag), oxygen supplementation.

HYDRATION CELLULAR

OSMOSIS.

Metabolic Efficiency

Cold air at high altitude has extremely low humidity, leading to rapid respiratory fluid loss. Cellular dehydration thickens the blood, increasing cardiac strain. BASSWOODS expeditions utilize trace-mineral electrolytes and warm water cycles to maintain blood viscosity and metabolic thermal regulation during high-altitude transits.

MINERAL CORE

Sodium, Potassium, Magnesium for neurological signal stability during exertion.

LOGISTICS

Insulated 1L flasks mandatory. Zero cold-water consumption above 12,000ft base hubs.

Safety First

Trekking Safety FAQ

How do we handle leeches in the rainforest treks?

Common in Lower Arunachal and Meghalaya. We provide 'Leech Socks' and use natural lime/tobacco repellents. Leeches are harmless but require technical removal to prevent secondary infection.

What is the emergency extraction protocol for remote treks?

We use satellite messaging (where legal/permitted) or runner-relays. Extraction is usually via pony-stretcher to the nearest 4x4 road-head, from where our dedicated response vehicles take over.
