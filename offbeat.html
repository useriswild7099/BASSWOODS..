Unmapped Frontier Directory

DIBANG VALLEY

The least densely populated district in India. Terrain is defined by high rhododendron ridges and complex Siang-Dibang river systems.

Technical Isolation

Access via Roing-Anini road. 4x4-Low range territory. Satellite comms mandatory for all BASSWOODS squads deployed here.

WALONG SECTOR

The easternmost point of India. A site of immense historical significance and breathtaking alpine pine forests.

The First Light

The trek to Dong at 03:00 AM is a technical endurance test in sub-zero conditions. Gear requirements: -20°C thermal systems.

MEGHALAYA ABYSS

A subterranean frontier of limestone river caves and the highest unmapped waterfalls of the Mawphlang ridge.

Expedition Support

Offbeat Prep FAQ

How do we manage communication?

Satellite GPS messengers (Garmin InReach) are standard for SOS. We provide detailed offline map caches (GPX) for all guests.

Is custom catering available?

Yes. Our mobile kitchen specialises in ethnobotanical cuisine using locally foraged ingredients for an authentic taste of the wild.

What is the fitness requirement for offbeat sectors?

While our 4x4s do the heavy lifting, some sectors (like the Dong Valley trek) require Level-3 cardio endurance. We recommend a 2-week incline-walk regimen prior to deployment.

How long does permit processing take?

Inner Line Permits (ILP) for Indian citizens take 72 hours. Protected Area Permits (PAP) for foreign nationals require 3-4 weeks. We require clear ID scans at least 10 days before the mission start date.

What are the medevac protocols?

Our squads carry Level-2 trauma kits and satellite-mesh for SOS. For high-isolation extractions, we maintain active links with regional medical transport hubs in Shillong and Pasighat.

When is the best season for offbeat deployment?

October to April is the primary window. Most river-cave systems and high rhododendron ridges are strictly inaccessible during the monsoon (June-September) due to hydrological risks and terrain instability.
