EXPEDITION FAQ

LOGISTICS & PERMITS
Do I need special permits for Northeast India?

Yes, Indian citizens require an ILP for Arunachal, Nagaland, and Mizoram. Foreigners need RAP/PAP. We coordinate all permits post-booking.

Where is the best pickup point?

Our primary vehicle hub is Guwahati (GAU). We offer remote drop-offs in Shillong or Kohima for custom itineraries.

CAMPING & FACILITIES
What toilet facilities do you provide?

Western-style private toilets at festival hubs. Eco-friendly portable systems for high-wilderness expeditions.

Is charging available at campsites?

Yes, communal high-capacity charging docks and solar-backup units are standard across all Basswoods hubs.

SAFETY & SUPPORT
Medical emergency protocols?

Level-2 med-kits and satellite-sync coordinates are standard. Direct links with regional centers for rapid evacuation.

FRONTIER HANDBOOK

WILDERNESS PROTOCOLS
The Zero Footprint Rule

Strict 'Leave No Trace' policy. All waste is extracted; environments are restored to pre-camp states.

High-Altitude Acclimatization

Mandatory rest-sync days above 3,000m. Oxygen saturation and hydration monitored by expedition leads.

The Tribal Code & Etiquette

Respect sacred groves. Always accept tea. Always ask before photography. You are a guest in a living community.

Emergency Gear Essentials

Mandatory: Multi-tool, headlamp, thermal base-layers, and water filtration. Personal readiness is technical readiness.

Connectivity & Sat-Link

Lead vehicles carry satellite-mesh for emergency weather and coordinate synchronization. Expect complete digital detox.

Mountain Sickness (AMS) Protocol

'Ascend slow, sleep low.' We carry localized O2 canisters and AMS medication. Alert guide if persistent headache occurs.
