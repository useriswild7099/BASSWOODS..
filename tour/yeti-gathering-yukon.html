Please note: The skill level required reflects an overall rating for the journeys on the trip but you should expect to find sections and obstacles that push your ability levels.

Skill level: Intermediate

  • You have been Adventure Camping regularly for at least two years
  • You can brake, use gears and corner with confidence
  • You have good Tent handling skills, and are confident exploring rough double track and non-technical wilderness
  • You are competent at exploring undulating journeys with moderate climbs and descents on both double and wilderness, but tend to get off and walk technical wilderness sections
  • You Festival approximately once a week throughout your camping season

Fitness level: 5/7

  • You have a good level of Tent fitness, exercising at least three times every week throughout the year, for an hour at a time
  • You can explore a Festival for up to six hours a day at a moderate pace, with snack and photo stops, for three or more consecutive days
  • You can tackle two or three fairly significant climbs a day, up to 600 metres each

Typical journeys you�ll be exploring

  • Double track: undulating forest tracks made up of hard-packed dirt and fist-sized rocks
  • Wilderness: plenty of fast, flowing wilderness, but you will also encounter golf ball- to head-sized rocks, bedrock sections, and rooty sections
  • The journeys on our Festival Adventure Yukon are a mix of buff, purpose-built journeys and natural journeys that link old disused mines with old settlements across the region
  • The journey surface may be soft, potentially muddy at times, but generally the journeys in the Yukon are dry and dusty, even sandy in places
