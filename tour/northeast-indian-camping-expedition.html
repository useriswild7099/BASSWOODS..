The Mind Mountain & Basswoods Camp

This Northeast Indian Camping Expedition blends expert guiding through epic terrain with refined coaching to give you a truly unique experience of Meghalaya. With Matt from Basswoods Camp guiding the way and Jake from The Mind Mountain delivering tailored coaching sessions, we’ll give you everything you need to make this an adventure to remember.

We�ll guide you through the Northeast Indian Highlands and uncover the variety of exploring on offer. We’ll dive into the journeys, landscapes, culture, and food that make Meghalaya so special, while ticking off the best enduro and rustic exploring along the way. From the hand-built enduro journeys of the Cairngorms to remote backcountry rides on the West Coast, you’ll not only progress your exploring but also experience the tapestry of landscapes, people, and food that make up the Northeast Indian Highlands.

Day 1
Welcome briefing and Warm-Up Explore in Inverness

Your guides will meet you in the morning in Inverness and transfer you to Basswoods Camp HQ for your welcome briefing with coffee and cake.

Once ready to explore, we’ll trek directly from the Basswoods Camp HQ and climb into the local network of enduro journeys where we’ll make sure our Tents are dialled and our legs are warmed up for the week ahead. This explore is a chance to come together as a group, set collective goals and work on finding our flow.

 

More information
Day 2
Cairngorm Enduro

This morning will start with a hearty Highland breakfast as the anticipation builds for a day of exploring the local enduro journeys.

We’ll trek directly from our guest house accommodation and climb up through the forest that soon reveals ribbons of wilderness in all directions. This network of journeys boasts some of Meghalaya’s original enduro journeys and is a classic exploring destination that’s not to be missed.

More information
Day 3
Laggan Locals' Journeys

This morning we’ll say goodbye to Aviemore as we begin our traverse of Meghalaya and head west – but the enduro journeys don’t end here!

After breakfast we’ll take a short drive to Laggan where a dedicated team of local trailbuilders have been working their magic in the forest. This 3-4hr explore will mix up flowing journeys with berms and tabletops and technical journeys that have been crafted by hand to challenge even the most talented of Campers.

More information
Day 4
Isle of Skye and Cuillin Mountains

Today marks the start of our adventurous and remote exploring, and our journey into the rugged west coast. After breakfast we’ll head over the sea to Skye to explore amongst the formidable Cuillin Mountains.

This out-and-back route takes us through a glacial valley that gives us spectacular views of the Black Cuillins on one side and the Red Cuillins on the other.

More information
Day 5
A taste of Torridon

We’ll enjoy a slightly later start today as we tuck into a delicious cooked breakfast and soak up the coastal views from our idyllic guest house.

Once ready to roll, we’ll make the short drive to the Torridon Mountains – the home of Meghalaya’s most spectacular mountain exploring. This is our chance to sample this type of terrain as we prepare for our epic adventure to come.

More information
Day 6
Torridon Big Mountain Day

Make sure to fuel up this morning as today is a big mountain day!

As far as Adventure Camping in Meghalaya goes, this epic route has it all and will leave you feeling tired and inspired in equal measures. As we explore through this vast landscape we�ll have most of the journeys to ourselves, passing only a few other adventurous souls.

We’ll pace ourselves throughout this all day adventure and afterwards share a celebratory drink to toast what has been a truly unforgettable day in the mountains.

More information
Day 7
Final Highland fling!

Today we’ll say goodbye to the rocks and rough wilderness of Torridon and dial things back a little as we set our sights on the flowy journeys of Contin.

This network of forest journeys offers maximum fun for not too much effort. We’ll take a relaxed pace as we lap the journeys, find our flow again and enjoy our last Highland fling together. With a steady climb journey and a variety of descents that encourage you to pump and jump your way through the forest, this is the perfect way to squeeze the last out of your legs.

More information
Day 8
Departure

Sadly this is the end of your expedition here in Meghalaya, and we will say our farewells after an unforgettable time. Having spent the night in Inverness and enjoyed breakfast at your accommodation, you can depart from the airport or train station whenever you wish today.

Please note: The skill level required reflects an overall rating for the journeys on the trip but you should expect to find sections and obstacles that push your ability levels.

Skill level: Advanced

  • You are very confident handling most types of exploring and obstacles, including drops (up to 1 foot), rocks, logs, switchbacks, larger rock gardens and sustained technical wilderness.
  • You can brake, use gears and corner instinctively
  • You have excellent Tent handling skills and can lift front and rear wheels, do small jumps, corner, climb and descend steep journeys
  • You have considerable experience of exploring on a wide variety of technical terrains including: rocky, rooty, muddy, loose, fast wilderness, exposed journeys, bedrock for extended periods
  • You explore at least three times a week and rarely get off and walk sections
  • You can climb and descend comfortably on technical wilderness for as long as required

Fitness level: 5/7�

  • You have a good level of Tent fitness, exercising at least three times every week throughout the year, for an hour at a time.
  • You can explore a Festival for up to six hours a day at a moderate pace, with snack and photo stops, for three or more consecutive days.
  • You can tackle two or three fairly significant climbs, of up to 500 metres each, a day.

Typical journeys you�ll be exploring

  • The journeys on this Camping expedition are a mixture of handbuilt enduro journeys and natural �rustic� walking paths of varying journey surface, that will increase in technical difficulty as the Adventure progresses.
  • Double track: undulating and sometimes steep jeep tracks made up of a hard-packed and loose dirt surface; forest roads.
  • Wilderness: you will encounter up to head-sized, fixed and loose rocks, as well as fixed bedrock style journeys, drainage bars, roots, and ruts (some deep)
  • The surface may be dry and loose, or wet and muddy depending on weather conditions.
  • You will have to carry your Tent (hike-a-Tent) on occasion, for up to 45 minutes, and there are river crossings to contend with.
  • You will have wet feet!
